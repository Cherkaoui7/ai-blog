**The Relatable Pain**

Do you often find yourself standing in front of an empty fridge at 5 pm, wondering what to make for dinner? Or perhaps you've been scrolling through social media, feeling guilty about the takeout habit you're trying to break? Meal planning can be overwhelming, especially when you're on a tight budget.

**The Problem**

Most people fail at meal planning because it requires too much time and effort. You need to browse recipes, make a shopping list, go grocery shopping, and then cook meals that your family will actually eat. It's no wonder many of us give up after a week or two.

**A Simple Solution**

Meal planning doesn't have to be complicated. Here are some simple habits to help you save time and money:

* Plan around staples: Choose 3-5 main ingredients (e.g., chicken, rice, beans) and build meals around them.
* Use a meal planner app: Find an app that helps you plan and organize your meals for the week.
* Cook in bulk: Prepare large batches of food on weekends or days off to save time during the week.

**Actionable Steps**

1. **Start small**: Plan one meal per day, three times a week, for two weeks. Gradually increase the number of meals as you become more comfortable with the process.
2. **Use a meal planner app**: Try an app like Yummly or Mealime that helps you plan and organize your meals.
3. **Stock up on staples**: Buy ingredients in bulk to save money and reduce food waste.
