## Pros and Cons

### Monarch Money
#### Pros:

* Flexible budgeting approach to accommodate uneven monthly spending
* Supports category budgets and tracking
* Offers free tier with premium features starting at $9.99/month

#### Cons:

* Limited features in the free version
* Some users find the interface not as intuitive as others

### [YNAB](https://www.ynab.com/?via=YOUR_AFFILIATE_ID) (You Need a Budget)
#### Pros:

* Zero-based budgeting approach helps you prioritize expenses
* Robust tracking and reporting features
* Available on multiple platforms, including desktop, mobile, and web

#### Cons:

* Paid-only model can be a barrier for some users
* Can be overwhelming to set up initially

### [EveryDollar](https://www.ramseysolutions.com/ramseyplus/everydollar?via=YOUR_AFFILIATE_ID)
#### Pros:

* Simple zero-based budgeting approach based on Dave Ramsey's principles
* Free tier available with premium features starting at $1.50/month
* Integration with other Ramsey tools, such as Budgeting Course and Debt Snowball Calculator

#### Cons:

* Limited automation in the free version
* Some users find the app too simplistic for complex budgets

### [Goodbudget](https://goodbudget.com/?via=YOUR_AFFILIATE_ID)
#### Pros:

* Envelope-style budgeting approach helps you plan spending categories
* Available on multiple platforms, including mobile, desktop, and web
* Offers a free tier with premium features starting at $7/month

#### Cons:

* Limited tracking and reporting features in the free version
* Some users find the interface not as modern or user-friendly as others

## Final Recommendation

For most households with irregular income, **Monarch Money** is the best option. Its flexible budgeting approach allows you to adapt to changing financial situations without sacrificing visibility into your spending. However, if you're looking for a more hands-on zero-based budgeting experience, **YNAB** might be a better fit.
